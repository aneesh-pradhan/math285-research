\begin{abstract}
This paper investigates series RLC circuits, comprising a resistor~(R),
inductor~(L), and capacitor~(C) connected in series, through the lens of
ordinary differential equations~(ODEs).
We derive and analyze first-order ODEs governing simplified RC and RL
sub-circuits, as well as the second-order ODE describing the full RLC system.
Analytic solutions are obtained for all three configurations, and the role of
resistance in controlling circuit damping is examined by varying component
parameters.
Our central research question asks: \emph{How do resistors influence the
damping and stability of series RLC circuits as described by ODEs, and what
are the practical implications for engineering applications such as filters
and oscillators?}
Results confirm that the resistor governs whether the circuit is overdamped,
critically damped, or underdamped, with direct consequences for signal
processing and control system design.
\end{abstract}
